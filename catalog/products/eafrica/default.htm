Microsoft® Encarta® Africana 2000
Discover the rich heritage of persons of African descent.

Microsoft Encarta Africana brings the richness and diversity of the black experience to life. Powerful learning tools such as on-location videos and thousands of comprehensive articles provide an indispensable learning tool and an essential part of your reference collection.

Watch the African world’s traditions unfold with Microsoft Encarta Africana!


Microsoft Encarta Africana Web Site
  • More in-depth content! 3,600+ authoritative articles, 200 sidebars, 2,900+ multimedia features, and 800 web links!
  • Library of Black America! A rare literary collection of books, slave narratives, novels, and poems written by Africans and African Americans from 1773 to 1918. Includes W. E. B. Du Bois', "Souls of Black Folk."
  • Music Timeline! Consulted by Quincy Jones. Experience the evolution of African American Music from ragtime to hiphop a new addition to the existing Africana Timeline feature which covers historical topics from 4 million B.C.E. to the present.
  • Civil Rights Movement Chronology! Relive 125 key defining moments in the U.S. Civil Rights Movement, such as Martin Luther King¹s inspiring "I Have A Dream" speech.
  • Africana on Camera! Exclusive Celebrity videos featuring perspectives on various Africana topics. Encarta Africana 2000 includes a new video from Colin Powell "African Americans in the U.S. Military."
  • Virtual Tours! Great Zimbabwe and the Serengeti.
